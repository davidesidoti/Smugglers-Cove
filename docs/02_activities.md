# 02 - Activities & Progression Curve

## Guiding principle

Activities are not unlocked in order of increasing profit. They are unlocked in order of **increasing gameplay complexity**, where each one introduces a new *verb* that builds on the previous ones. Every activity has a legal side and an illicit side, otherwise the dual track splits into two parallel games (see `01_overview.md`). Upgrades change *how* you play, never just *how much* you earn.

Each new activity **stratifies** on top of the old ones rather than replacing them. By the endgame you are still selling at the market, still repairing ships, still forging weapons - but now at scale and with the complexity of a network to manage. The complexity curve and the risk curve rise together.

## Phase 1 - Market (verb: SELL)

Starting activity. Teaches the base loop without complexity: buy stock, display it, serve customers, manage prices.

- **Legal side:** provisions, food, nautical gear for passing ships. Steady, low margin, the backbone.
- **Illicit side:** the cover par excellence. Stolen or smuggled goods blend in among legal stock. This teaches the founding concept: **legal volume is the screen behind which you hide illegal volume.**
- The Market never stops being relevant - it stays the spine of laundering for the whole game. Generates little Heat.

## Phase 2 - Ship Repair (verb: REPAIR / SERVE on commission)

Gameplay complicates pleasantly. No longer selling items off a shelf - you accept *commissions*: a ship arrives damaged, you assess the job, perform it, deliver.

- Introduces **time management** (repairs take in-game hours, you manage a queue), **diagnosis** (figure out what's needed), and **materials** (have the right stock).
- **Legal side:** standard repairs for merchants and fishermen. Honest, low-margin work.
- **Illicit side:** repair wanted pirate ships "no questions asked," and especially make *illegal modifications* (reinforcements, hidden contraband compartments, repairs to ships the Navy is hunting).
- This is where the Pirate relationship starts to matter, and the first activity to generate meaningful Heat.

## Phase 3 - Weaponry (verb: ARM - and risk spikes)

Deliberately later because it is the most dangerous. Selling and making weapons is what the Navy watches worst. Not given early - it is *earned* once the player can handle risk.

- Introduces the **production** verb (you don't just resell, you manufacture, with a materials supply chain).
- **Legal side:** registered weapons, licensed sales, supplying the Navy itself (also a way to court them).
- **Illicit side:** unregistered weapons, sales to pirates, clandestine manufacture.
- Sharply raises the Heat stakes. Separates the cautious player from the ambitious one.

## Phase 4 - Tavern / Inn (verb: GATHER INFO & WEAVE RELATIONSHIPS)

Unlocked around mid-game. Not a pure sales business - it is the **nerve center of relationships.**

- Gather rumors (tips on incoming ships, imminent inspections, planned heists), meet characters, receive illicit jobs under the table, recruit NPCs.
- **Legal side:** food and lodging, modest steady income.
- **Illicit side:** the information market and the meeting point for trafficking.
- Transforms the game from "I run shops" into "I run a network," fusing the social and economic pillars. Becomes your **intel** system: knowing about an inspection in advance is worth gold (feeds directly into the Heat system, see `04_heat.md`).

## Phase 5 - Expansion (verb: COORDINATE a network)

The activity endgame is not "open the tenth shop on the same island" - it is a jump in scale: a **second port on another island**, your own trade routes, or fleets of ships you send out to trade and smuggle on your behalf.

- The verb becomes **logistics and delegation**: you no longer manage points of sale, you manage a distributed network you cannot physically be present for all at once.
- Reconnects directly to midgame Lever 1 (scarcity shifts onto your time/attention) and Lever 4 (unlocks that change the game). You go from shopkeeper to *criminal entrepreneur with an empire to control*.

## The shape of the curve

Verb progression: SELL -> REPAIR-on-commission -> PRODUCE -> GATHER-INFO/RELATIONSHIPS -> COORDINATE. Each phase stratifies, never replaces. Risk rises in lockstep: Market generates little Heat, illicit Ship Repair more, Weaponry a lot, Expansion multiplies the points where you can be caught.

## Gating (what unlocks what)

Activities unlock through **factions and characters**, not just money - otherwise a rich player skips the progression and buys the ending.

- Weaponry requires a certain level with the Guild (for supply chains) or the Pirates (for the black market).
- The second port requires having built enough relationships to have contacts on the other island.

This keeps economic progression intertwined with the social and risk pillars: you can't buy the late game with a sack of coins, you must have *lived* the relationships that unlock it.
